  Current Events

Eureka! 2024 Pitching Competition

We organized a pitching competition at our college to promote entrepreneurship and provide early-stage startups with an opportunity to participate in Eureka! 2024. Twenty startups were selected to present 2-minute pitches followed by 3-minute Q&A sessions with judges from the startup community. Feedback sessions were held for participants, and the winner’s pitch deck was submitted to E-Cell IIT Bombay for fast-tracked evaluation in the Zonals. The event concluded with a felicitation ceremony for the jury and a summary speech.

Screen Stories Challenge

Our Screen Stories Challenge invited participants to create a post about their favorite movie that motivates them and write a few words explaining its impact. The challenge received an overwhelming response with over 30 entries this year. After careful evaluation, the top three winners were announced, and certificates were awarded to celebrate their exceptional contributions.

LinkedIn Presentation

We created an impactful PowerPoint presentation on leveraging LinkedIn for networking, both for E-Cell and personal branding. The presentation covered useful tools such as RocketReach, Apollo, and Streak to enhance networking efforts. We also included the LinkedIn profiles of our team members within the presentation and captured photos of the session.

Create your Social Media presence

Our E-Cell has successfully launched its Instagram page to build a dynamic social media presence, connecting with startup enthusiasts and keeping followers updated on the latest in entrepreneurship. Through engaging posts covering news on startups, entrepreneurial events, and tributes to influential figures, we aim to inspire and inform our community.

Linkedin Blog

A series of LinkedIn blogs was created, focusing on startup tips, founder success stories, industry insights, and daily motivation for entrepreneurs. The series consisted of posts every alternate day, with at least four posts shared, engaging the community and inviting them to share their stories.

Head Start task

We encouraged our team to inspire friends from other colleges to start their own Entrepreneurship Cells (E-Cells). This included hosting a virtual icebreaker meeting with our team and posting about it on social media to spread the entrepreneurial spirit to more campuses.

Brand your E-Cell (B)

We crafted a strong brand identity for our E-Cell by developing a clear Vision and Mission statement that reflects our long-term goals and actions to achieve them. Additionally, we created a unique logo and tagline that encapsulate the spirit of our organization, ensuring a cohesive identity that aligns our members with a unified purpose.

Define the roles

To enhance efficiency and coordination within our E-Cell, we created a structured portfolio system, defining clear roles, responsibilities, and the required number of members for each role. We also introduced our team under the "Know Your Team" theme to strengthen our community connections.

Know your motivation

Each team member articulated their personal motivation for joining the E-Cell by writing at least 100 words about what drives them, particularly through challenging times. This activity offered insight into the team’s shared entrepreneurial spirit and commitment.

Make Your Faculty Advisor

We officially appointed a faculty advisor for our E-Cell to provide valuable guidance and support. An official letter was drafted and approved by the institution's leadership. Regular meetings are held with the advisor to discuss upcoming activities and evaluate past events.

What is the Problem?

We identified three entrepreneurship-related problems on campus and organized a group discussion with at least 10 members to address these issues. A poster was created to invite participants, and during the discussion, meaningful solutions were explored and documented.
